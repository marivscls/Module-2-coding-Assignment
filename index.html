Famous Philosophers

Plato

Plato (427-347 BC) was a Greek philosopher of antiquity, considered one of the main thinkers in the history of philosophy. He was a disciple of the philosopher Socrates. His philosophy is based on the theory that the world we perceive with our senses is an illusory, confused world. The spiritual world is higher, eternal, where ideas really exist, which only reason can know.

Socrates

Socrates (470-399 B.C.) was a philosopher of ancient Greece, the first thinker of the trio of ancient Greek philosophers, which included Plato and Aristotle, to establish the philosophical foundations of Western culture. "Know thyself" is the essence of all his teaching. Socrates was born in Athens, Greece, in 470 BC.

Immanuel Kant

Immanuel Kant (1724-1804) was a German philosopher, founder of "Critical Philosophy" - a system that sought to determine the limits of human reason. His work is considered the cornerstone of modern philosophy.
